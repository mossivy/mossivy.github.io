Core Principles

Quick Summary

Students should treat AI as a supervised assistant: use it for generation, feedback, and editing while retaining authorship, disclosing tool use, and following institutional rules.

Core Ethical Principles

AI use should be grounded in clear ethical values

Transparency

State where and how AI was used. Clear disclosure preserves trust.

Academic Integrity

Preserve authorship. You remain responsible for all work.

Skill Development

Use AI to support learning, not replace critical thinking.

Equity

Consider unequal access and avoid unfair advantages.

Human Oversight

Always verify and take responsibility for AI content.

Governance

Follow institutional policies. Ask when unsure.

What's Acceptable?

Distinguish supportive uses from substitutions

Permitted

  • Idea generation: Brainstorming and outlining
  • Grammar & style: Editing for clarity
  • Concept explanation: Understanding difficult topics
  • Feedback: Suggestions while you're the author
  • Research: Finding sources with verification

Prohibited

  • Direct submission: AI essays as your own
  • Fabrication: Fake citations without checking
  • Test bypass: AI for unaided assessments
  • Hidden paraphrasing: Minor edits to conceal
  • No verification: Unvetted AI output
